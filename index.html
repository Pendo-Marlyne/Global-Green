Executive Summary

Renewable deployment accelerated worldwide in 2025, with solar and wind leading new capacity additions. Geothermal remains the most efficient baseload option where geology allows, while policy and grid investment continue to shape regional outcomes.

Annual Growth

Capacity and investment

Year-over-year growth in installed capacity reflects falling technology costs and stronger climate commitments. Utility-scale projects and distributed generation both contributed to the expansion.

Regional highlights

Emerging markets scaled solar fastest; mature markets emphasized grid integration and storage co-location.

Resource Types

Solar and wind

Photovoltaic and onshore wind dominate new build thanks to modularity and short construction cycles.

Geothermal and other sources

Geothermal offers high capacity factors where resources are proven; hydro and biomass remain important in specific contexts.

Key Takeaways

  • Solar posted the strongest relative growth among major sources in the 2025 snapshot.
  • Wind maintains a strong efficiency position and steady double-digit growth.
  • Geothermal leads on efficiency but shows modest growth, reflecting siting and capital intensity.
  • Diversifying the mix improves resilience as electrification and demand rise.
